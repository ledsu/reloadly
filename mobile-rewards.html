Mobile phone rewards across industries

Brand Loyalty 2.0

Build loyalty and engagement by offering top-up values for phone bills. As the new currency of the information age, mobile credit is incredibly valuable. Mobile phone rewards are also a frictionless incentives strategy -- there are no loyalty cards, vouchers or QR codes. This is used in industries as diverse as banking, FMCG, social media and mobile gaming.

Survey Participation

Encourage higher response rates for your surveys and studies. Successfully soliciting participation from people living in remote markets is difficult. However, by tapping the world’s vast prepaid mobile market, you can offer airtime and data top-up rewards. Prepaid customers especially love gifts that help pay their phone bills. This is commonly used by pollsters, NGOs and academic institutions, as well as companies conduct market research.

Commercial Partner Programs

Effectively incentivize small-scale commercial partners such as resellers to boost sales with low-cost mobile rewards. Incentivizing small entrepreneurs is normally very complicated because the ticket sale per visit is so small. However, by offering low-cost mobile rewards, these mom-and-pop stores are able to consistently sell more products. FMCG and other mass-market industries commonly use mobile rewards in this way.

Employee Rewards and Incentives

Human resources departments use mobile rewards to incentivize behavior that fits their company culture. Offering mobile phone top-ups as a reward is especially valuable for employees that travel a lot and use their personal phone to call home. Mobile phone rewards are also effective incentives for encouraging employees to participate in voluntary events and studys. HR departments across Latin America, Africa and Asia increasingly offer this type of incentive program.
